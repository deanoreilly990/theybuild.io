The five gaps

Five gaps every business has —
and doesn’t know it.

If your business doesn’t show up when someone asks ChatGPT for a recommendation, you don’t exist to that customer. These are the gaps we find in nearly every audit we run.

01

You’re invisible to AI search.

When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity or Google AI for a recommendation in your category, your name doesn’t come up. Not buried — absent.

02

Your product data isn’t agent-ready.

AI shopping agents can’t read your catalogue the way they read your competitors’. The structured signals they need are missing, or wrong, or written for a 2014 search engine.

03

Your reviews aren’t being indexed.

The signals AI uses to rank you — third-party citations, schema, structured Q&A, fresh reviews — sit in places you’ve never looked.

04

You have no AEO baseline.

You can’t measure what you can’t see. Right now, you can’t see your AI visibility — so you can’t tell if anything you do is working.

05

You have no one watching the shift.

AI search changes weekly. Nobody on your team is paid to track it. By the time you notice you’ve slipped, you’ve slipped for a quarter.

“This isn’t a report about what you’re doing wrong. It’s a map of where the AI landscape is moving and the five specific things to fix before your competitors do.”

That sentence is the brand. Andre had been told by three agencies that he needed to “leverage AI” — a phrase that helps no one. He didn’t need to leverage anything. He needed to know whether his catalogue was readable by a shopping agent (it wasn’t), whether his reviews were being indexed in the places AIs cite (they weren’t), and whether anyone was watching the shift on his behalf (nobody was).

We wrote ten pages. Andre kept the document. We didn’t keep the relationship — at his request, the engagement ended at the audit. That ending is the proof point. The audit is a real deliverable, not a free sample on the way to a contract.
